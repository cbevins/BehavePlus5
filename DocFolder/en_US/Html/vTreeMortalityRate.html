Probability of Mortality

Probability of mortality is the likelihood that a tree will be killed by a fire. The equation used to calculate probability of mortality depends on the mortality tree species. There are 12 mortality equations, which variously include bark thickness, tree crown length scorched and/or tree crown volume scorched.

Probability of mortality can be interpreted as the probability of an individual tree dying or it can be multiplied by the number of trees on a site to estimate the number of trees that will die.

Probability of aspen mortality is calculated by a different model and is available by selecting the western aspen special case fuel model in SURFACE.

Bark thickness is estimated from the tree species and D.B.H. using equations from First Order Fire Effects Model: FOFEM 4.0 User's Guide (Reinhardt, E.D., R.E. Keane, and J.K. Brown, 1997).

New fire mortality equations are from the JFSP Report #05-2-1-105 Delayed Tree Mortality following Fire in Western Conifers (Hood, S., S. Smith, D. Cluck, E. Reinhardt, and K. Ryan, 2008)
